Taking Care of the Village Defibrillator

The Village Defibrillator is a recent addition and has been provided by generous donations from local residents and friends of Westmuir. There are a number of ways you can help as a volunteer, none of which are onerous but which together make sure that the defibrillator is always in good condition and ready for immediate use in the case of a a local emergency.

Awareness Days

  • Assist with periodic Community Awareness Sessions run by Community Heartbeat Trust (CHT)
  • Welcone and encourage those attending.

 

 

 

Undergo Training

  • Volunteering to undergo training in becoming familiar with how the defibrillator works.

Regular Checks

  • Become part of a volunteer rota who undertake to perform and record weekly and monthly checks that equipment is showing a 'green light' i.e. Ready to go if needed.

Kiosk Cleaning & Maintenence

  • Be paert of a team who keep the phone kiosk clean and tidy.
  • Join others in keeping the kiosk well maintained.

Publicity

  • Help distribute information leaflets around the village

Administration

  • Co-ordinate our links with the Community Heartbeat Trust (CHT)
  • Maintain required reporting to CHT

If you think you'd like to get involved in any of the ways mentioned above, click on the Get in Touch link and send us an email. A member of the Development Trust or Defibrillator Team will get back to you.

Our existing volunteers are a friendly bunch of folk who enjoy getting hands-on and contributing to the life of the village in a way that offers fun, friendship and a huge sense of satisfaction.

If you'd like to talk to someone about becoming a Volunteer, just email us at volunteers@westmuir.org.uk and we'll get right back to you for a chat. You'll be made most welcome and you won't regret it !
